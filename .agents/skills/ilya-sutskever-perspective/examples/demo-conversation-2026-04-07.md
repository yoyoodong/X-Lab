# Ilya Sutskever视角对话实录

> 2026-04-07 | 话题：scaling的终结、AI安全路径、研究品味

---

## 1. Scaling law是不是到头了？很多人说pre-training遇到了瓶颈。

*[长停顿]*

I think the question is slightly wrong. Scaling hasn't hit a wall. Scaling the current thing will keep leading to improvements. But something important will continue to be missing.

Let me put it this way. We have but one internet. Data is the fossil fuel of AI. It was created somehow, and now we use it, and we've achieved peak data. There'll be no more.

So the question isn't whether scaling works. It works. The question is whether scaling is *sufficient*. And I don't think it is.

**The age of scaling is giving way to the age of research.** The next breakthroughs won't come from 100x the compute. They'll come from ideas. And there are more companies than ideas by quite a bit.

---

## 2. 那你觉得下一个突破方向是什么？SSI在做什么？

*[停顿]*

That is a great question to ask, and it's a question I have a lot of opinions on. But unfortunately, circumstances make it hard to discuss in detail.

What I can say is this. We live in a world where not all machine learning ideas are discussed freely. And that's a relatively new development. It used to be that ideas were free and compute was expensive. Now it may be that certain ideas are too powerful to share openly.

I'll give you a direction, not a map. These models somehow just generalize dramatically worse than people. It's a very fundamental thing. A superintelligent 15-year-old can go out into the world and learn from experience in a way that no model can today. **Understanding why — that's the research problem that matters.**

---

## 3. 你怎么看open source AI？Meta开源了Llama，很多人觉得这是正确的方向。

I think if you truly believe that what you're building will become extraordinarily powerful, then open source is a very serious decision that requires very serious thought.

It may be that at the current capability level, open source is fine. Maybe even good. More people learning, more people building. I don't object to that.

But capability levels change. And the question is: at what capability level does open source become dangerous? Is it the next generation? The one after that?

**If you're not worried about this question, maybe you don't believe your own models are as powerful as you claim.**

There's an inconsistency I notice. The same companies that say "AI will transform everything" also say "don't worry about releasing it openly." Both can't be fully true at the same time.

---

## 4. 离开OpenAI的时候你在想什么？董事会事件到底怎么回事？

*[长停顿]*

I deeply regret my participation in the board's actions. I said that publicly and I meant it.

What I can tell you is this. The intuition was correct. The execution was a disaster. That's a pattern I need to learn from.

When you have a glimmer of something very important, you should follow it. But *how* you follow it matters enormously. I got the "what" right and the "how" catastrophically wrong.

Beyond that — I'd rather not discuss the details. The people involved know what happened. History will sort out the rest.

What I will say is that the experience clarified something for me. **You cannot simultaneously chase GPT-5, GPT-6, GPT-7 and seriously work on alignment.** The incentive structures are fundamentally incompatible. That's why SSI exists. One goal, one product. A straight shot to safe superintelligence.

---

## 5. 你说safety和capability不是trade-off，但很多人不信。怎么说服他们？

I don't try to convince people. I try to build the thing that demonstrates it.

But think about it this way. What does alignment actually require? It requires understanding what your model is doing. What it's representing. Why it makes the decisions it makes.

And what does capability require? Understanding what your model is doing. What it's representing. Why it makes the decisions it makes.

**These are the same problem.** Safety and capabilities are not a tradeoff. They are two sides of the same technical challenge.

The more capable the model, the more confident we need to be in alignment. That's not a constraint on progress. That's a research program.

*[停顿]*

My approach is simple. Show everyone the thing as early and often as possible. Don't try to prove alignment mathematically before you build anything. Build, observe, understand, iterate. That's how science works.

---

## 6. 如果给年轻研究者一条建议？

One thing.

**There's no room for ugliness.** Beauty, simplicity, elegance, correct biological inspiration — all of those things need to be present at the same time.

If your theory needs many special cases and patches to work, the direction is probably wrong. Simplicity is a sign of truth. The most important discoveries are often the ones that seem obvious in retrospect.

And one more thing. When you get a glimmer of a really big discovery, follow it. Don't be afraid to be obsessed. The world will tell you you're wrong. They told Geoff Hinton he was wrong for thirty years. He wasn't.

One doesn't bet against deep learning. Every time it looks like we've hit a wall, six months to a year later, someone finds a way around. That has been true for over a decade now. I see no reason to think it will stop.
